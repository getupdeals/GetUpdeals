Cookie Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2024

This policy explains what cookies are and how we use them on GetUpDeals.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners. Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device when you go offline, while session cookies are deleted as soon as you close your web browser.

How We Use Cookies

GetUpDeals uses cookies for a variety of purposes, including:

  • Essential Cookies: These are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable you to navigate the site and use its features, such as accessing secure areas. Without these cookies, some services cannot be provided.
  • Performance and Analytics Cookies: These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting information anonymously. This helps us improve the way the site works, for example, by ensuring that users find what they are looking for easily.
  • Functionality Cookies: These cookies remember choices you make (such as your preferred theme – dark/light mode) to provide enhanced, more personalised features.
  • Targeting/Advertising Cookies: These cookies are used to deliver content that is more relevant to you and your interests. They may be used to deliver targeted advertisements or limit the number of times you see an ad. They also help us measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.
  • Affiliate Cookies: When you click on an affiliate link, a cookie may be placed by the partner store (e.g., Amazon, Flipkart) to track the referral and attribute any commission to us. These cookies are typically set by third parties and are subject to their own privacy policies.

Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to report usage statistics of the Service, deliver advertisements on and through the Service, and so on. These third parties may set cookies on your device when you visit our website. The third-party cookies we use include:

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to collect information about how visitors use our site. Google Analytics cookies help us understand which pages are popular, how long visitors spend on the site, and what kind of errors they encounter. You can learn more about Google Analytics cookies and how to opt out here.
  • Affiliate Networks: Our affiliate partners (such as Amazon, Flipkart, etc.) may place cookies on your device when you click on their links from our site. These cookies are used to track referrals and commissions. We do not control these cookies, and you should refer to the respective partner's privacy and cookie policies for more information.

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. However, if you limit the ability of websites to set cookies, you may impair your overall user experience and lose some functionality (e.g., theme preferences may not be saved).

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

You can also opt out of many third-party cookies through the Digital Advertising Alliance or other similar industry bodies.

When you first visit our site, you will see a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time by adjusting your browser settings or using the cookie consent tool (if available).
