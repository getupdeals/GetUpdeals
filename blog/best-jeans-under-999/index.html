How to Measure for Perfect Fit:

1

Waist Measurement

Measure around your natural waistline (above hips). Add 1 inch for comfort.

2

Inseam Length

Measure from crotch to ankle bone. Most jeans come in 30", 32", 34" lengths.

3

Hip Measurement

Measure around the fullest part of your hips for proper fit.

üßµ Denim Fabric Guide

Understanding denim types helps you make better choices:

100% Cotton

Traditional Denim

Durable but less stretchy. Softens with wear. Best for straight/slim fits.

Cotton + Elastane

Stretch Denim

98-99% cotton + 1-2% elastane. Comfortable with shape retention.

Wash Types

Dark vs Light Wash

Dark: Formal, slimming. Light: Casual, shows wear faster.

Denim Weight Guide:

Lightweight (8-10 oz)

Summer friendly, more comfortable but less durable.

Medium (10-12 oz)

All-season, balanced comfort and durability.

Heavy (13+ oz)

Winter wear, very durable but stiff initially.

üß∫ Jeans Care & Maintenance

Proper care extends your jeans' life significantly:

Wash Less Frequently

Wash every 4-5 wears. Spot clean minor stains.

No Fabric Softener

Breaks down fibers. Use vinegar instead for softness.

Inside Out Wash

Protects color and reduces fading.

Air Dry Only

Dryer damages elastic fibers and causes shrinkage.

Breaking In New Jeans

Wear them at home for a few hours before first wash. The fabric will mold to your body shape.
